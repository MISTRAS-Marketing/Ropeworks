New MISTRAS Ropeworks New York City Office

We are pleased to announce that East River Rigging (ER2) has joined the Ropeworks® Division of MISTRAS. With a long-standing working relationship with ER2, we know this will be a great fit both culturally and strategically.


East River Rigging Technician, Adam Schletsky. The Peninsula Hotel, NYC

MISTRAS Ropeworks will grow its rope access operations on the East Coast through one of the region’s most respected industrial rope access service providers. ER2 brings to our team expertise in building inspection and maintenance, installation of fall protection and netting systems, and experience with the local petrochem and telecom industries. Joining the MISTRAS family will help our New York team bring advanced inspection and asset protection solutions to local customers.

Co-founders Nat Russell and Jason Parmer will continue to manage New York City operations from their Brooklyn office. Please don’t hesitate to contact us if you have any questions. We look forward to continuing to serve our customers.

Team Leader Summits



Team Ropeworks

The Ropeworks division recently held our first of several Team Leader Summits at our training center in Reno, NV. This is an opportunity for our Field Team Leaders (Rope Access Level 3’s) to come together to share best practices and sharpen their leadership skills to better serve their team members and our customers.

As our team continues to grow throughout North America and internationally, we plan to hold several Team Leader Summits in each major geographic region.

One of the highlights of these gatherings is the heckling and friendly competition created through the Rope Access Olympics.

Featured Book: The Happiness Advantage


Each quarter our team members can choose to participate in the Business Book Club (BBC). The BBC began in 2007 to help our team build leadership and business skills to match the needs of our growing business. We are pleased to share some of our favorites with you periodically. Our current book is:

The Happiness Advantage: The Seven Principles of Positive Psychology That Fuel Success and Performance at Work, by Shawn Achor

Maintaining a positive attitude in the face of adversity and change has long been one of our guiding principles. Shawn Achor applies research from the relatively new field of positive psychology to the workplace. The research points to the fact that a positive culture helps drive positive results.

We look forward to sharing and applying what we learn to continually improve how we work and deliver for our customers.
